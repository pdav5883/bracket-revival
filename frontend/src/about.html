About bracket-revival

A celebration of the premier friends and family sport picking competition on the US calendar, bracket-revival is the scientifically-proven best way to experience March Madness without the sadness of seeing all your Final Four teams wiped out in the first weekend. Before every round, you get to re-do all your picks, so your bracket is never busted!

Navigating Pages

To play bracket-revival you'll need to Join a Game. If it's currently early March you'll be able to join one of several games. Otherwise you can join any of the Historical competitions for previous years to test your March memory.

Once you join a game, you'll make picks on the Picks page, where you click to select winners of games in each round.

Check how you're doing against fellow competitors on the Scoreboard page. From there you can look at how your Bracket looks across rounds and games. You can click on each game to see your picks across your various rounds of picks, as well as see how many points you've won for each game.

Formatting

All of the brackets you see on the Picks and Bracket pages use a Javascript library called Bracketry, which is the most-capable and best-documented library I could find for bracket visualization. Bracketry is focused on tennis tournament brackets, but has plenty of flexibility to make it usable as a College Basketball bracket picking interface. Free software is amazing!

The rest of the formatting for bracket-revival is built on the Bootstrap framework. I've barely scratches the surface of what Bootstrap offers, but the pieces that are used here provide a much better UI than the rest of BLR.
