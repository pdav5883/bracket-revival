bracket-revival Rules

In bracket-revival you get the chance to pick a fresh bracket after every round of the tournament. In each bracket you'll pick a winner for all remaining games, even if your earlier picks were terrible. If your earlier brackets correctly pick the winner for later rounds, you'll score more points.

Here are the rules, which will be explained in more detail below:

  1. You'll submit a bracket with picks for all remaining games after each round.
  2. Each bracket you submit will start with the correct teams, regardless of previous picks.
  3. In order to score points for a game, you must pick the winner correctly in your most recent bracket [1 point].
  4. You score bonus points for a game if you also pick the winner correctly in earlier brackets [2x points per bracket].

More Details

You might say this seem overly complicated, and you wouldn't be wrong, but it's a game about games so that's probably ok. If you take nothing else away from the rules, just remember:

  • Get as many picks right as you can
  • The earlier you make a correct pick, the more points you will get
  • Stick with any picks that are still alive from previous brackets (they will pre-populate)

Let's start with some bracket-revival vocabulary:

  • Round: a set of games in the tournament. For example: First Round, Second Round, Sweet 16, Elite 8, Final Four, and Championship.
  • Bracket: the picks you make before each round of the tournament. For example your Second Bracket will contain picks for all games from the Second Round through the Championship.

When you make your picks for a bracket, your starting point will be all the teams that are still alive, regardless of how good or bad your previous bracket was. For example, if you get every First Round game wrong in your First Bracket, you'd be starting with a blank slate in your Second Bracket, and be picking between the teams that actually made it to the Second Round. Revival!

Game Logistics

There are two types of competitions here at bracket-revival: live competitions and historical competitions. Live competitions are ones that are played along in real time with the actual NCAA tournament, while historical competitions are open all year round to test your NCAA Tournament recollection.

For both types of competition you will join a game via the New Player page. For a live game, you will receive an email with a personalized link to pick your First Bracket prior to the beginning of the First Round. After each round ends you will receive an email with a new link to pick your Next Bracket. Make sure to pick your bracket before the next round begins! You'll be able to check the competition Scoreboard and see your Brackets at any time, but you won't be able to edit your picks.

For historical competitions, you can go as slow or as fast as you want making picks. There is no email link, you just need to visit the Picks page and select your year/competition/name.

How to Read Your Picks

When you click on your email link for live competitions or navigate to Picks for historical competitions, you'll see a screen that looks something like this:

A check mark next to a team's name means that is your current selection for who will win that game. The number in the blue box is the number of points you will win if you pick that game correctly. In the case of Arkansas over Gonzaga, it means that you picked Arkansas to win this Sweet 16 game in your First and Second Brackets, as well as having them selected to win in this bracket.

Games without a box/check are games you haven't picked, like the Carolina vs UCLA matchup. You just need to click/tap one of the teams to select it as the winner. If there is no team showing for one/both sides of a game, like the Purdue Elite 8 game, that means you need to make picks in the previous round to populate down the bracket.

Your picks will automatically populate with any picks you made from previous brackets that are still alive. You should not change these picks unless you have a really good reason, as you'll have fewer points available to win if you do.

Depending on your device, you'll see anywhere from one to all rounds visible. Make sure you use the PREV ROUND / NEXT ROUND buttons at the top of the bracket to navigate to other rounds. Remember, you need to pick all remaining games, including the Championship, each time you submit a bracket.

And finally, don't forget to hit the Submit button for your picks!

Calculating Points

Thankfully you don't have to worry about this since the site does all the point calculation for you, but if you're interested in how points are calculated, keep reading.

You get points for a game when it is played. In order to get any points at all you must pick the game correctly in your most recent bracket. This means that for a Second Round game, you must pick the winner correctly in your Second Bracket. For an Elite 8 game, you must pick the winner correctly in your Fourth Bracket. That gets you one point.

If you got the point, take a look at your previous bracket. If you picked the winner correctly in that bracket, then multiply your point total for the game by two. Keep going backwards in your bracket, and each time you picked the game correctly, multiply your points be two.

So if the game is a Second Round game that you picked correctly in your Second Bracket you get 1 point. Now take a look at your First Bracket. If you picked the winner correctly there, then you get 2 points! If not, you just get the 1 point.

For an Elite 8 game (Fourth Round), you could potentially look through your Fourth, Third, Second, and First brackets, and if you got them all correct you'd have 8 points for the game. The max number of points available per game in a round is:

  • First Round: 1 point
  • Second Round: 2 points
  • Sweet Sixteen: 4 points
  • Elite Eight: 8 points
  • Final Four: 16 points
  • Championship: 32 points

Note that there are 32 points available for every round.

As a concrete example, let's say for 2023 (UConn won it all) you picked UConn to win the Championship in your first five brackets, then changed your mind in the Sixth Bracket and decided they would lose to San Diego State. You would get 0 points! If you weren't so foolish and stuck to your guns with UConn in your Sixth Bracket, you'd get 32 points!

And now for a final gotcha that gets buried at the bottom of the rules page. When calculating the bonus multiplier, as you go back through previous brackets you stop counting when you see a pick other than the true winner. Back to the UConn example above, let's say you picked UConn to win the Championship in your first Four Brackets, then get cold feet in your Fifth Bracket and picked someone else, then went back to UConn in your Sixth Bracket. You would get one point for the Sixth Bracket, take a look at your Fifth Bracket and see not UConn, and get no bonus multiplier. It doesn't matter that you picked UConn before that!
